History

Mercedes-Benz traces its origins to Karl Benz's
first internal combustion engine in a car, seen in the
Benz Patent Motorwagen financed by Bertha Benz's dowry
and patented in January 1886 and Gottlieb Daimler and his
engineer Wilhelm Maybach's conversion of a stagecoach, with
the addition of a petrol engine, introduced later that year.
The Mercedes automobile was first marketed in 1901 by Daimler
Motoren Gesellschaft (DMG).
